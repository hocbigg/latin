# Latin - Resources

## Vocabulary

- **[Textkit Greek and Latin Learning Tools](https://www.textkit.com/)**  
  Extensive collection of free downloadable public domain grammars, readers, and vocabulary tools for building core word lists in both languages.

- **[Dickinson College Core Vocabulary Lists](https://dcc.dickinson.edu/vocab/core-vocabulary)**  
  Frequency-based core vocabulary lists for Latin and Greek, essential for prioritizing high-utility words.

- **[Perseus Word Study Tool](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/morph)**  
  Morphological analyzer and vocabulary lookup for words encountered in texts, supporting both Latin and Greek.

## Grammar

- **[Open University: Getting Started on Classical Latin](https://www.open.edu/openlearn/history-the-arts/getting-started-on-classical-latin/content-section-0)**  
  Free interactive course introducing Latin grammar fundamentals with explanations and exercises.

- **[Open University: Getting Started on Ancient Greek](https://www.open.edu/openlearn/history-the-arts/getting-started-on-ancient-greek/content-section-0)**  
  Free beginner course covering the Greek alphabet, basic grammar, and early structures.

- **[latintutorial YouTube Channel](https://www.youtube.com/user/latintutorial)**  
  Detailed video explanations of Latin grammar topics, from basics to advanced syntax.

- **[Ancient Greek Tutorials (Perseus)](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus:text:1999.04.0007)**  
  Online grammar reference and tutorials for Ancient Greek, with examples from texts.

## Reading & Comprehensible Input

- **[Perseus Digital Library](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/)**  
  Vast collection of Greek and Latin texts with tools for word-by-word analysis, translations, and morphological parsing to support comprehensible reading.

- **[Dickinson College Commentaries](https://dcc.dickinson.edu/)**  
  Annotated editions of select Latin and Greek texts with notes, vocabulary, and multimedia aids for intermediate readers.

- **[The Latin Library](https://www.thelatinlibrary.com/)**  
  Comprehensive archive of public domain Latin texts across genres, ideal for progressive reading practice.

- **[First Thousand Years of Greek Project](https://opengreekandlatin.github.io/First1KGreek/)**  
  Open-access corpus of Ancient Greek texts with tools for reading and exploration.

## Writing & Composition

- **[Textkit Forums Composition Exercises](https://www.textkit.com/greek-latin-forum/viewforum.php?f=5)**  
  Community-posted exercises and corrections for Latin and Greek prose composition.

- **[Bradley's Arnold Latin Prose Composition (Archive.org)](https://archive.org/details/bradleysarnoldla00braduoft)**  
  Classic public domain exercises for practicing Latin composition, adaptable for self-study.

## Listening

- **[ScorpioMartianus YouTube Channel](https://www.youtube.com/c/ScorpioMartianus)**  
  Extensive spoken Latin (and some Ancient Greek) content, including stories, dialogues, and readings for immersion.

- **[Found in Antiquity YouTube Channel](https://www.youtube.com/c/FoundinAntiquity)**  
  Comprehensible input videos with spoken Ancient Greek stories and readings, supported by visuals.

- **[Latinitium Spoken Latin Playlist](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLU1bR9Z5Q0G9jX4oW2vF0w7oZ5Z5Z5Z5)**  
  Curated collection of spoken Latin audio and video resources for pronunciation and listening practice.

## Cultural & Historical Context

- **[Open University: Discovering Ancient Greek and Latin](https://www.open.edu/openlearn/history-the-arts/discovering-ancient-greek-and-latin/content-section-0)**  
  Free course exploring the languages in their cultural and historical settings, with links to Greco-Roman world.

- **[MIT OpenCourseWare: The Ancient World - Rome](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/21h-132-the-ancient-world-rome-spring-2017/)**  
  Lecture videos and materials providing historical context for Latin literature and society.

- **[Perseus Art & Archaeology Collections](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/collections)**  
  Images, maps, and artifacts integrated with texts to illustrate cultural and historical backgrounds.

- **[Dickinson College Commentaries Essays](https://dcc.dickinson.edu/)**  
  Introductory essays on authors and works, embedding texts in their historical and cultural contexts.
